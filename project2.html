OVERVIEW

Problem

While juggling moving, work, and other commitments, finding a roommate can be hard. In the end, you might have to settle for the first person that agrees to your terms which potentially lead to getting scammed.

Solution

Introducing coHabit . coHabit is the new and best solution for all your roommate finding problems. coHabit requires users to provide references and link their professional LinkedIn account in order to start using the platform. After that, users can request people to take individual quizzes, set up a phone call, and meet for coffee securely through this platform. Finding a roommate has never been so secure and easy!

USER RESEARCH

Survey

So while I had a problem and a predicted solution to my problem, I knew I needed to better understand what the user would really want out of this product. So I created a user survey with these goals in mind:

  • 1. To understand what important information people want to know when finding a roommate
  • 2. What are the other roommate finding platforms that have been used and why
  • 3. What features would be useful in a potential roommate finding app

The results from 20 participants were:

  • -78% of participants prefer to select people vs being matched
  • -Main concerns were finding someone who is reliable and meets all of their roommate expectations (age, gender, salary, etc)
  • -Top features that would be useful in the app: Messaging, Sending a quiz, and setting up a meeting

After taking these results, I knew I need to create an app that find the perfect roommate, in an easy and best way. The app will provide references and an easy intuitive way for users to interact with each other.

User Stories & User Flows

From the results of the survey and personas, I created a list of what I believed users would want to accomplish with my product. I prioritized certain tasks as high, medium, and low, and also for a minimum viable product (which included signing up, signing in, account settings, etc).

I converted the user's stories into user flows to visualize how the user would accomplish those stories with this platform. Separate flows demonstrate how new and returning users would be welcomed into the platform. I created flows for how a user would message or send another user a quiz. I also created a flow showing how a request for a reference would be sent to someone.

Branding

Word Association & Mind Mapping

Using the results from the survey and personas, I knew that I needed to create a brand that was secure and also would give satisfaction.

I began my brainstorm with word-association exercises to help come up with a name for the platform. Finally, I was able to narrow down the name for my roommate finder platform to coHabit. coHabit is a simple, intuitive, but all-inclusive platform.

Logo Development

While developing the logo, I thought about the best way to represent my name coHabit. I wanted something to show that this platform is fun and memorable, but also professional. I sketched out and then created some mockups of potential logos. I conducted a preference test to decide which logo was preferred.

Style Guide + Colors

After brainstorming ideas and names, picking the fonts, and creating the logo, I picked the colors which I felt would represent the brand the best. I knew I wanted to keep it one monochromatic scheme with one color with different shades, as it looks more professional and clean. I decided to go with blue, as blue is the color of trust.

WHAT I LEARNED

coHabit seeks to solve a legitimate problem that many people face regularly when finding a roommate. While I had several strong feelings about the concept and design, the most important lesson learned was regarding checking my own biases. While I may believe that I'm the ideal user and target of a product it is crucial to learn more about those around me facing the same problem. Time and time again during the process of designing coHabit I was loudly corrected by my users. It was humbling, but also allowed me to design with confidence knowing decisions were made with the voices of users.
